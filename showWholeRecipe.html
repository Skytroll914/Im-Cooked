Nasi Lemak

Nasi Lemak is a traditional Malaysian dish, often considered the national dish. It consists of fragrant rice cooked in coconut milk, served with sambal, crispy anchovies, toasted peanuts, boiled egg, and cucumber slices.

Cook Time: 30 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of jasmine rice
  • 1 cup of coconut milk
  • 1 cup of water
  • 2 pandan leaves, tied into a knot
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup dried anchovies (ikan bilis), cleaned
  • 1/4 cup roasted peanuts
  • 4 boiled eggs, halved
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste

For the Sambal:

  • 1/2 cup dried chili peppers, soaked in warm water
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 2 shallots
  • 1 tablespoon shrimp paste (belacan)
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste, mixed with 1/4 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
  2. In a rice cooker, add the rinsed rice, coconut milk, water, salt, and pandan leaves. Cook until the rice is done.
  3. For the sambal, blend the soaked chili peppers, garlic, shallots, and shrimp paste into a smooth paste.
  4. Heat oil in a pan and fry the chili paste over medium heat until fragrant. Add tamarind juice, sugar, and salt, and cook until the sambal thickens.
  5. Fry the cleaned anchovies in hot oil until crispy, then set aside.
  6. To serve, plate the coconut rice and top with sambal, crispy anchovies, roasted peanuts, boiled eggs, and cucumber slices.
  7. Enjoy your Nasi Lemak with a side of extra sambal for an added kick!

Char Kuey Teow

A popular Malaysian street food, Char Kuey Teow is a stir-fried flat noodle dish with shrimp, eggs, Chinese sausage, and chives, flavored with a mix of soy sauce and chili paste.

Cook Time: 20 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 200g flat rice noodles (kuey teow)
  • 100g shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 50g Chinese sausage, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 50g bean sprouts
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon chili paste
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Chopped chives for garnish
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a wok over medium heat. Add minced garlic and stir-fry until fragrant.
  2. Add shrimp and Chinese sausage to the wok and cook until the shrimp turns pink.
  3. Push the shrimp and sausage to one side of the wok, then pour in the beaten eggs and scramble until just set.
  4. Add the flat rice noodles to the wok, stirring everything together.
  5. Mix in soy sauce, chili paste, and fish sauce, ensuring the noodles are well-coated.
  6. Toss in the bean sprouts and stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste, then garnish with chopped chives before serving.

Rojak

Rojak is a popular Malaysian and Indonesian fruit and vegetable salad dish, known for its sweet and tangy sauce. It's a delightful mix of textures and flavors, often enjoyed as a snack or a light meal.

Cook Time: 10 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 cucumber, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 jicama, peeled and cut into matchsticks
  • 1 pineapple, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 1 green apple, sliced
  • 1 mango, peeled and sliced
  • 1/2 cup tofu puffs, sliced
  • 1/4 cup roasted peanuts, coarsely ground
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds, toasted

For the Dressing:

  • 3 tablespoons shrimp paste (belacan)
  • 2 tablespoons tamarind paste
  • 3 tablespoons palm sugar, grated
  • 1 tablespoon chili paste (optional)
  • 1/4 cup water
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the shrimp paste, tamarind paste, palm sugar, chili paste (if using), and water. Mix until smooth and well combined.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, toss together the cucumber, jicama, pineapple, green apple, mango, and tofu puffs.
  3. Pour the dressing over the salad and toss until everything is evenly coated.
  4. Sprinkle the salad with roasted peanuts and toasted sesame seeds.
  5. Serve immediately and enjoy your refreshing Rojak!

Cendol

This recipe focuses on the cendol jelly itself, not the entire dessert. You'll need to prepare coconut milk, gula melaka syrup, and shaved ice separately to complete the dish.

Cook Time: 20 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 100g mung bean starch
  • 100g water
  • 1/2 tsp alkaline water (optional)
  • Pandan extract or juice (for color)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine mung bean starch, water, and pandan extract. Mix until smooth.
  2. If using alkaline water, add it now and mix well.
  3. Bring a pot of water to a boil.
  4. Slowly pour the mung bean starch mixture into the boiling water while stirring constantly.
  5. Reduce heat and continue stirring until the mixture thickens and becomes translucent.
  6. Transfer the mixture to a piping bag fitted with a small nozzle.
  7. Pipe the mixture into a bowl of ice water to form cendol strands.
  8. Rinse the cendol strands with cold water to remove excess starch.
  9. Serve immediately or store in the refrigerator until ready to use.

Dumpling

This recipe focuses on the dumpling wrapper and a basic pork and vegetable filling. You can customize the filling to your preference.

Cook Time: 30 minutes

Ingredients for Wrapper:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup hot water
  • 1 tablespoon oil

Ingredients for Filling:

  • 300g ground pork
  • 1/2 cup chopped cabbage
  • 1/4 cup chopped carrots
  • 1/4 cup chopped onions
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

For the wrapper:

  1. In a large bowl, combine flour and hot water. Stir until a dough forms.
  2. Knead the dough for about 5 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  3. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  4. Divide the dough into equal-sized balls.
  5. Roll out each ball into a thin circle using a rolling pin.

For the filling:

  1. In a bowl, combine ground pork, cabbage, carrots, onions, soy sauce, sesame oil, salt, and pepper. Mix well.

To assemble dumplings:

  1. Place a spoonful of filling in the center of each wrapper.
  2. Fold the wrapper into half and press the edges together to seal.
  3. Crimp the edges to create decorative patterns (optional).

To cook dumplings:

You can boil, steam, or pan-fry the dumplings according to your preference.

Briyani

Note: This is a simplified version of briyani. For a more authentic flavor, consider using a biryani spice blend and additional ingredients like saffron and kewra water.

Cook Time: 1 hour

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 2 large onions, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece ginger, minced
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 4 cloves
  • 2 cardamom pods
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil
  • Yogurt (for marinating chicken)

Instructions:

  1. Marinate chicken pieces in yogurt, ginger, garlic, and salt for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Heat oil in a large pot. Add bay leaves, cloves, cardamom, and cinnamon.
  3. Add onions and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add marinated chicken and cook until browned on all sides.
  5. Add ginger, garlic, green chilies, and spices (garam masala, turmeric, coriander, cumin).
  6. Add enough water to cover the chicken and bring to a boil.
  7. Reduce heat and simmer until chicken is cooked through.
  8. Rinse basmati rice and soak in water for 30 minutes.
  9. Drain the rice and add it to the chicken pot.
  10. Increase heat and bring to a boil.
  11. Cover the pot and reduce heat to low. Simmer until rice is cooked and water is absorbed.
  12. Fluff the rice with a fork and serve hot.

Chicken Tikka Masala

Chicken Tikka Masala is a popular Indian dish known for its creamy, spiced tomato sauce. It features tender pieces of marinated chicken, making it a favorite around the world.

Cook Time: 45 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 500g chicken breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 2 teaspoons paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1 can (400g) tomato puree
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine yogurt, lemon juice, oil, cumin, paprika, coriander, turmeric, garam masala, and black pepper. Add the chicken pieces and marinate for at least 30 minutes, preferably overnight.
  2. In a large pan, heat oil over medium heat. Add onion, garlic, and ginger, and sauté until the onion is soft and translucent.
  3. Add the marinated chicken to the pan and cook until the chicken is browned on all sides.
  4. Stir in the tomato puree and cook for 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  5. Reduce the heat and add the heavy cream, stirring until the sauce is smooth and creamy.
  6. Simmer for another 10 minutes, adding salt to taste.
  7. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with rice or naan bread.

You Tiao

You Tiao, also known as Chinese crullers or doughnuts, are long, golden-brown strips of fried dough, often enjoyed as a breakfast snack or served alongside congee or soy milk.

Cook Time: 30 minutes (plus resting time)

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 3/4 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and sugar.
  2. Add water and vegetable oil to the dry ingredients. Mix until a dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough for 5-7 minutes until smooth. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 2 hours.
  4. Roll the dough into a long strip and cut it into 1-inch wide pieces.
  5. Take two pieces of dough and stack them on top of each other. Press a chopstick lengthwise in the center to make them stick together.
  6. Heat oil in a deep pan. Stretch the dough pieces slightly before frying them in hot oil until golden brown on both sides.
  7. Remove and drain on paper towels. Serve warm.

Rendang

Rendang is a rich and flavorful Indonesian dish made with slow-cooked beef, coconut milk, and a blend of spices. It is a classic dish known for its tender meat and aromatic flavors.

Cook Time: 2-3 hours

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg beef, cut into cubes
  • 2 cans (800ml) coconut milk
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 stalks lemongrass, bruised
  • 4 kaffir lime leaves
  • 2 turmeric leaves (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Sugar to taste

For the Spice Paste:

  • 6 shallots
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 2 inches ginger
  • 2 inches galangal
  • 5 dried chilies, soaked and deseeded
  • 2 teaspoons ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground fennel

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, combine all the ingredients for the spice paste and blend until smooth.
  2. In a large pot, heat oil and sauté the spice paste until fragrant.
  3. Add the beef cubes and cook until browned.
  4. Add coconut milk, lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, turmeric leaves (if using), salt, and sugar. Stir well.
  5. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer. Cook uncovered for 2-3 hours, stirring occasionally, until the beef is tender and the sauce is thickened.
  6. Adjust seasoning with salt and sugar. Serve with steamed rice.

Roti Canai

Roti Canai is a popular Malaysian flatbread that is crispy on the outside and soft on the inside. It is typically served with curry or dhal as a delicious breakfast or snack.

Cook Time: 1 hour (plus resting time)

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 cup condensed milk
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter or ghee
  • Oil for cooking

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix flour, salt, and sugar. Add water, condensed milk, and melted butter. Knead to form a soft dough.
  2. Divide the dough into small balls. Coat each ball with oil and let them rest, covered, for at least 2 hours.
  3. On a greased surface, flatten each ball and stretch it thinly into a large circle without tearing.
  4. Fold the edges of the circle inward to form a square or roll it up and coil it into a spiral.
  5. Heat a griddle or pan with a little oil. Cook each roti on medium heat until golden brown on both sides, pressing with a spatula.
  6. Serve hot with curry or dhal.
